Mobile Design

Deutsche Stiftung Denkmalschutz

A mobile app concept for the Deutsche Stiftung Denkmalschutz, designed to make cultural heritage more accessible through clear navigation, location-based discovery and app-specific features.

Year

2023

Category

Mobile App Concept

Focus

UX Research, Information Architecture, UI Design

Overview

This project was created for the Mobile Design module, with the task of developing an app concept for an existing organization. I chose the Deutsche Stiftung Denkmalschutz, Germany’s largest private initiative for monument preservation, and developed the concept in direct exchange with the organization, including access to its corporate design manual as the basis for the visual direction. The concept was created for two target groups, an older primary audience and a younger secondary audience, with the goal of offering a mobile experience that adds practical value beyond the existing website. Note: The app concept is fictional and was created solely for the purpose of this assignment. It is not an official project of the Deutsche Stiftung Denkmalschutz.

Approach

My approach combined the foundation of the existing brand identity with a clearly user-centered app structure. I designed the concept as an iOS application with a tab bar navigation and focused on three key areas: a home screen for relevant content, a map view for nearby monuments and an audioguide section for on-site exploration. Additional ideas such as image recognition were developed to make the experience more useful and intuitive, especially for older users and tourists. To support the concept, I worked with user research, card sorting and usability testing. The final designs were ultimately refined based on feedback.
